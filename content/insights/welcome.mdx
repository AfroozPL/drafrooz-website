I built this practice on a single observation: **the distance between the
research lab and the enterprise boardroom is the widest it has ever been,
and the cost of misjudging it is now strategic.**

In 2026, your competitors are not just deploying GenAI. They are deploying
it _well_ — or not. The difference is rarely the model. It is the rigour
of the decisions surrounding it: where to apply it, how to govern it, what
to measure, when to walk away.

## What you will find here

Short notes from the frontier — written for AI leaders who want
evidence, not buzzwords. Three pillars guide the writing:

- **Enterprise AI Strategy** — board-ready frameworks, build-vs-buy, ROI.
- **Healthcare Innovation** — responsible AI in clinical and operational settings.
- **IS & Business Analytics** — the curriculum question for the GenAI era.

Each post will be opinionated, grounded, and short enough to read on a
flight between Sydney and Singapore.

## A small commitment

> "AI is not a replacement — it is a multiplier for human expertise when
> deployed strategically."

Every post will end with one practical takeaway. That is the contract.
